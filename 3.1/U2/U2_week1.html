Grammar: Singular and Plural Nouns

▾
Singular noun — names one person, place, or thing.
Plural noun — names more than one. Add -s or -es; some are irregular.
RuleSingularPlural
add -sdog, room, tree, computerdogs, rooms, trees, computers
add -es (sh, ch, tch, x, s, ss)ash, inch, watch, fox, glassashes, inches, watches, foxes, glasses
consonant + y → change y to i + -eslady, copy, hobbyladies, copies, hobbies
irregular pluralsman, mouse, deer, goose, personmen, mice, deer, geese, people

Spelling: Syllable Pattern VC/V & V/CV

▾
VC/V — short vowel sound; split after the consonant following the short vowel.
e.g. lem/on, hun/dred, rab/bit, win/ter
V/CV — long vowel sound; split after the long vowel.
e.g. ba/by, ti/ger, mu/sic, ro/bot

Vocabulary Words 1–10

▾
digit 數字
(n) a number symbol from 0 to 9
finish 完成
(v) to complete something
salad 沙拉
(n) a food made from mixed vegetables
talent 天賦
(n) a natural ability or a skill someone is born with
defend 防禦；防衛
(v) to protect from harm; to say something right or true
equal 相等的
(adj) exactly the same; the same in number or amount
famous 有名的
(adj) well known, known by many people
human 人類
(n) a person; relating to people
minus 減
(v) to take away an amount; (adj) below zero
total 總數
(n) the whole amount

High-Frequency Words

▾
certain 肯定的
(adj) sure about something — Ethan was certain that the test answers were correct after checking carefully.
half 一半
(n) one of two equal parts of something — Mom cut the orange in half for us to share.
