IV. Reading Comprehension The Tale of Honest Tom

The Tale of Honest Tom

Honest Tom was known throughout the village for being a kind but sometimes careless boy. He often wandered through the woods, helping animals and picking berries, while forgetting to finish chores at home.

One sunny afternoon, Tom found a small injured fox near a stream. He carefully carried it home and tended to its wounds. His neighbors watched him work patiently, amazed at his kindness.

After the fox recovered, it ran back into the forest. Tom felt happy knowing he had helped. That evening, he realized he had forgotten to milk the cows and sweep the barn. He rushed to finish his chores before the sun set.

The next day, the villagers praised Tom for his helpfulness to the fox. They reminded him, however, to balance his time wisely. Tom learned that being kind was important, but he also needed to take care of his responsibilities at home.

From that day forward, Tom continued to help animals and neighbors, but he made sure his chores were done first. Everyone in the village admired his honesty and care.
