Common Nouns vs Proper Nouns

▾
Common noun — names any person, animal, place, or thing.
Proper noun — names a specific one. Always capitalize proper nouns.
CategoryCommon NounProper Noun
personwoman, teacher, doctorMs. Jen, Mr. Headington, Dr. Carver
author, illustratorSusy Pilgrim Waters
animaldogHusky, Poodle
catSnowball, Fluffy
placecityTaichung, Taipei
countryUnited States, Mexico
storeMcDonald's, 7-11
schoolTunghai University
thingcarToyota
book titleThe Cat in the Hat
daySaturday
month / holidayAugust, Thanksgiving

Vocabulary Words 1–15

▾
bounce 彈跳
(v) to move quickly up or back after hitting a surface
fountain 噴泉
(n) a structure that sends water into the air, often decorative
proud 自豪的
(adj) feeling good about oneself or someone else
thousand 千
(n) the number 1,000
power 力量；權力
(n) the ability to do something or make things happen; energy
shower 陣雨；淋浴
(n) a brief fall of rain; (v) to wash oneself quickly with water
appoint 指派
(v) to choose someone for a job or position
avoid 避開；避免
(v) to stay away from something; to prevent something from happening
choice 選擇；決定
(n) the act of picking one thing over another
foil 箔
(n) a very thin sheet of metal
moist 潮濕的
(adj) slightly wet, damp, not dry
noise 噪音
(n) a sound, especially loud or unpleasant
annoy 惹惱；打擾
(v) to bother, irritate, or make someone slightly angry
enjoy 享受
(v) to like doing something
loyal 忠誠的
(adj) always being faithful, true, or supportive

High-Frequency Words

▾
unit 單位；單元
(n) a single part or measure; a section in a book — I review one unit of math each day.
figure 數字；人物；想出
(n) a number or person; (v) to think or calculate — It took a while to figure out the answer.
