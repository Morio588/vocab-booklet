Subjects and Predicates

▾
Subject — tells who or what the sentence is about.
Predicate — tells what the subject does or is. The verb must agree with the subject.
Simple Subject
the main noun or pronoun (1 word)
Simple Predicate
the main verb (1 word / phrase)
Complete Subject
all words about who or what
Complete Predicate
verb + all words that tell what happens
Complete SubjectComplete Predicate
The men on the farmplant crops.
My best friendpractices before the game.
Her favorite artistsdisplay work in the museum.

▾

Vocabulary Words 1–14

▾
city 城市
(n) a place where many people live and work with many buildings, roads, and services
country 鄉下；國家
(n) land outside cities and towns; a nation with its own government
family 家庭
(n) people who are related to each other
fry 炒
(v) to cook food in hot oil
party 派對；政黨
(n) a fun event with food and games; a political group
penny 一分錢
(n) a small coin worth one cent; a very small amount of money
pony 小型馬
(n) a small horse
spy 間諜；查看
(n) a person who secretly watches or reports on others; (v) to secretly watch or collect information
patch 小片土地
(n) a small area that is different from its surroundings
pitch 投，扔；音調、音高
(v) to throw something; (n) the tone of a sound that can be high or low
inch 英吋
(n) a unit of length equal to 2.54 centimeters
dish 碗盤；菜餚
(n) a plate for food; a type of food prepared in a special way
glass 玻璃杯；玻璃
(n) a container for drinking; a hard, clear material used for windows or containers
dress 洋裝；著裝
(n) clothing for girls or women; (v) to put on clothing

High-Frequency Words

▾
story 故事；樓層
(n) something told or written about real or imaginary events; a level of a building — Noah wrote a story about dragons.
draw 繪製；吸引；抽籤
(v) to make a picture; to pull or attract something; to choose something randomly — Jen drew a card from the box to choose the class helper.
